Can we predict if an infant will be born with low birthweight?

Low birth weigth of an infant can have serious helth health effects on it in its later life. World Health Organization defines Low Birth Weight (LBW) as a birth weight of an infant of 2,499 g or less, regardless of gestational age. The birth weight of a baby is notable because very LBW babies are 100 times more likely to die compared to normal birth weight babies. A mother's aged, BMI, prior live births, and race; along with her tobacco and alcohol habits, air pollution, stress, etc. have been identified as factors that causes for LBW. This final project attempts to predict the weight of a baby, based on various factors recorded after the birth of an infant.
